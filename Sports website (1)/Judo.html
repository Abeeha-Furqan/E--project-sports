Players & Team Game

Players:

In judo, two opponents battle each other for the object of beating the other person with honor. In order to beat one's opponent, a player must obtain more points than the other by being awarded points.


Is it a team Game?

Judo is a competitive individual and team sport. Judo players, or judoka, compete directly against other players and medals or trophies are typically awarded for first, second, and third place winners. Some tournaments also feature teams whose members compete against each other.

Important Facts

Facts:

Judo is both a sport and Martial Art As a sport, Judo marked its entry into the Olympics in 1964. It took four years for men to compete in Judo as an Olympic event in 1968. For women, it was only until 1994, when the first Female Judo Olympic was held. Further, in 1988, Judo was added as a Paralympic event.

FAMOUS PLAYERS

David Douillet

David Donald Hubert Roger Douillet is a French politician and retired judoka. Douillet was born in the city of Rouen. Standing at 1.96 meters and weighing 125 kilograms, he won the judo heavyweight gold medals in the 1996 and 2000 Summer Olympics in Atlanta and Sydney.

Teddy Riner

Teddy Pierre-Marie Riner is a French judoka. He has won ten World Championships gold medals, the first and only judoka to do so, and three Olympic gold medals. He has also won five gold medals at the European Championships.

Tadahiro Nomura

Tadahiro Nomura is one of the most famous judo competitors in Japan. He is the only judoka in the world who has won three individual Olympic gold medals in a row, all in the extra lightweight division.

Masato Uchishiba

Masato Uchishiba is a Japanese judoka who won the gold medal in the men's under 66 kg division at the 2004 Summer Olympics in Athens, Greece, and at the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing, China.
